## 1. INTRODUCTION TO ICICI PRICING STRATEGY

ICICI Commercial Bank maintains a premium pricing strategy reflecting our value-added services, advanced digital infrastructure, and personalized relationship management. Our pricing is designed for large corporate clients requiring comprehensive commercial banking solutions with 24/7 support and dedicated relationship managers.

This catalogue represents our standard pricing structure. Actual pricing for individual clients may vary based on relationship value, transaction volumes, credit exposure, and negotiated terms. All clients are subject to our standard terms and conditions unless explicitly modified by written agreement.

All fees are quoted in Indian Rupees (INR) and are exclusive of applicable Goods and Services Tax (GST). Current GST rate is 18% and will be charged separately on all services. Fees are subject to annual escalation as specified in Section 8 of this catalogue.

## 2. CASH MANAGEMENT SERVICES

### 2.1 Account Maintenance and Management

**Current Account Maintenance Fee:** INR 3,500 per month

The monthly account maintenance fee covers account management, daily statement delivery via online portal, customer service support during business hours (9 AM - 6 PM IST), basic online banking portal access, regulatory compliance including KYC and AML monitoring, and account monitoring for suspicious activities.

Premium accounts with average monthly balance exceeding INR 50,00,000 may be eligible for relationship-based fee waivers subject to relationship manager approval. Multi-currency accounts attract an additional fee of INR 1,000 per month per additional currency maintained.

**Daily Statement Delivery:** INR 250 per statement (if email delivery requested)

Daily account statements are available on the online portal at no additional charge. Clients requesting email delivery of daily statements in PDF format are charged INR 250 per statement sent via email. Statements include opening balance, all transactions with timestamp and details, closing balance, and available balance after outstanding checks and pending transfers.

**Monthly Reconciliation Reports:** INR 1,200 per report

Comprehensive monthly reconciliation reports providing transaction-level detail with balance aging analysis, transaction-wise break-up by category such as deposits, withdrawals, transfers, interest earned if applicable, and fees deducted. Reports are delivered within 5 business days of month-end via online portal or email.

**Additional Custom Reports:** INR 2,000 per report

Clients may request additional custom reports beyond the standard monthly reconciliation including weekly cash position reports showing daily balances and net flows, customer-wise transaction reports for receivables tracking, vendor-wise payment reports for payables management, and custom MIS reports with pivot tables and analysis. Each custom report request is charged separately.

### 2.2 Cash Handling and Deposit Services

**Cash Deposit Processing:** INR 5 per INR 1,000 deposited (minimum INR 100 per deposit)

Cash deposits are processed through the Bank's branch network during business hours. The Bank charges a processing fee calculated as INR 5 per INR 1,000 of cash deposited with a minimum charge of INR 100 per deposit transaction. Large cash deposits exceeding INR 10,00,000 require 24-hour advance notice to the branch for currency availability planning.

**Cheque Deposit Processing:** INR 25 per cheque (for clearing)

Cheque deposits are processed through the clearing house network. The Bank charges INR 25 per cheque submitted for clearing. Clearing time is typically 2 business days for local cheques and 3-5 business days for outstation cheques depending on the location of the issuing bank. Cheques returned unpaid are charged an additional return fee of INR 500 per cheque.

## 3. DOMESTIC PAYMENT PROCESSING SERVICES

### 3.1 Automated Clearing House (ACH) Transactions

**ACH File Origination Fee:** INR 650 per file

The Bank processes ACH files submitted by clients for bulk payment processing including salary payments, vendor payments, dividend payments, and refunds. The file origination fee of INR 650 per file covers file validation, format verification, IFSC code confirmation, and submission to the RBI ACH network for settlement.

ACH files must be submitted in the Bank's specified format (NACH or CSV) with complete beneficiary details including name, account number, IFSC code, and amount. Files submitted before 12:00 PM IST are processed same-day (T+0) subject to network availability; files submitted after 12:00 PM are processed next business day (T+1).

**ACH Transaction Fee:** INR 3.50 per transaction

In addition to the file origination fee, the Bank charges INR 3.50 per individual transaction processed within the ACH file. If a client submits an ACH file containing 500 transactions, the total charge is INR 650 (file fee) plus INR 1,750 (500 transactions × INR 3.50) for a total of INR 2,400.

Volume discounts are available for high-volume clients processing more than 10,000 ACH transactions per month. Clients exceeding this threshold may contact their relationship manager for negotiated per-transaction rates.

### 3.2 Real-Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) Transactions

**RTGS Processing Fee:** INR 75 per transaction

RTGS is used for large-value fund transfers exceeding INR 2,00,000 with real-time settlement through the RBI RTGS system. The Bank charges INR 75 per RTGS transaction covering message preparation, RTGS network submission, settlement coordination with the beneficiary bank, and confirmation delivery to the client.

RTGS transactions settle in real-time with funds reaching the beneficiary within minutes of submission during RTGS operating hours (9:00 AM to 4:30 PM IST on business days). The Bank provides instant confirmation once the transaction is successfully settled. RTGS transactions exceeding INR 10,00,00,000 require additional authorization from the Bank's treasury team.

### 3.3 National Electronic Funds Transfer (NEFT) Transactions

**NEFT Processing Fee:** INR 35 per transaction

NEFT is used for standard-value fund transfers up to INR 10,00,000 with batch settlement through the RBI NEFT network. The Bank charges INR 35 per NEFT transaction covering transaction validation, NEFT network submission, batch processing, and settlement coordination.

NEFT transactions settle in hourly batches during NEFT operating hours (8:00 AM to 7:00 PM IST on business days) with settlement typically completing within 30 minutes to 2 hours of submission. The Bank provides confirmation once the transaction is successfully settled. NEFT is available 24/7 for online submissions with next-available-batch processing.

### 3.4 Cheque Processing and Clearing

**Cheque Processing Fee:** INR 45 per cheque

The Bank processes cheques issued by clients for payment through the clearing house network. The Bank charges INR 45 per cheque covering clearing house submission, bank-to-bank clearing coordination, settlement, and confirmation delivery. Local cheques clear in 2 business days; outstation cheques clear in 3-5 business days.

Cheques returned unpaid by the beneficiary bank (due to insufficient funds, account closed, signature mismatch, etc.) are charged an additional return handling fee of INR 500 per returned cheque. Stop payment requests for issued cheques are charged INR 300 per cheque.

### 3.5 Standing Instructions

**Standing Instruction Setup Fee:** INR 500 (one-time)

Clients may authorize standing instructions for recurring payments to specific beneficiaries on fixed dates (weekly, monthly, quarterly, or annually). The Bank charges a one-time setup fee of INR 500 per standing instruction covering instruction recording, beneficiary verification, schedule setup, and system configuration.

**Standing Instruction Monthly Maintenance Fee:** INR 500 per month

The Bank charges a monthly maintenance fee of INR 500 per active standing instruction covering automatic execution on scheduled dates, reconciliation, failure handling, and reporting. Standing instructions are executed automatically on the scheduled date without requiring client authorization for each transaction.

If a standing instruction fails (due to insufficient balance, account frozen, etc.), the Bank charges a failure fee of INR 200 per failed attempt. The standing instruction remains active and the Bank will retry on the next scheduled date. Clients may modify or cancel standing instructions at any time with 3 business days advance notice.

## 4. INTERNATIONAL PAYMENT SERVICES

### 4.1 Cross-Border Wire Transfers (SWIFT)

**SWIFT Wire Transfer Fee:** INR 950 per transaction

The Bank facilitates cross-border fund transfers through the SWIFT network for remittance to beneficiaries in foreign countries. The Bank charges INR 950 per international wire transfer covering SWIFT message preparation and transmission, correspondent bank coordination, fund settlement in destination country, and transaction tracking.

Processing time is typically 1-3 business days depending on the destination country, correspondent bank processing time, and local clearing procedures. The Bank provides SWIFT message copy (MT103) as proof of payment. Urgent SWIFT transfers with same-day or next-day settlement are charged an additional expedited fee of INR 500 per transaction.

### 4.2 FATCA Compliance Screening

**FATCA Screening Fee:** INR 100 per international remittance

For all outbound international remittances, the Bank performs mandatory FATCA (Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act) compliance screening to verify that the payment beneficiary does not trigger reporting requirements under U.S. tax regulations. The Bank charges INR 100 per international transaction for FATCA compliance verification.

FATCA screening includes checking beneficiary name and account details against OFAC (Office of Foreign Assets Control) sanctions lists maintained by the U.S. Treasury Department, verifying beneficiary tax residency status, maintaining FATCA certification documentation, and filing annual compliance reports with Indian tax authorities as mandated by law.

### 4.3 Correspondent Bank Charges

**Correspondent Bank Charge Recovery:** Actual charges plus 8% handling fee

Correspondent banks (banks in the destination country that facilitate fund settlement) impose their own charges for processing international payments. These charges vary by destination country, currency, and correspondent bank policies. The Bank recovers the actual correspondent bank charges from the client plus an 8% handling fee.

Correspondent bank charges are typically deducted from the remittance amount (unless the client specifically requests "charges borne by remitter" option). The Bank provides an estimate of correspondent charges prior to executing the transaction and confirms actual charges once received from the correspondent bank.

### 4.4 Foreign Exchange Conversion

**Foreign Exchange Markup:** 0.75% on spot rate

For international payments requiring currency conversion (converting INR to foreign currency or vice versa), the Bank applies a markup of 0.75% on the spot exchange rate published by the Bank. The markup is added to the exchange rate used for converting the remittance amount.

For example, if the Bank's published USD spot rate is INR 83.00 per USD and the client is remitting USD 10,000, the Bank applies the rate of INR 83.6225 per USD (83.00 × 1.0075) for a total INR amount of INR 8,36,225. The Bank publishes spot rates on its online portal updated every hour during business hours.

Forward contracts for hedging foreign exchange risk on future transactions are available with separate pricing based on forward points and tenor. Clients may contact the Bank's treasury team for forward contract pricing.

## 5. TRADE FINANCE SERVICES

### 5.1 Letters of Credit (Documentary Credits)

**Letter of Credit Issuance Fee:** INR 18,000 per LC

The Bank issues Letters of Credit (documentary credits) on behalf of clients as a payment guarantee to suppliers/beneficiaries for import and export transactions. The LC issuance fee of INR 18,000 per LC covers LC document preparation, term verification for compliance with UCP 600 (Uniform Customs and Practice for Documentary Credits), SWIFT transmission to the advising bank, and documentation management.

The issuance fee applies to LCs with validity up to 180 days from date of issuance. LCs with longer validity (181-365 days) are charged an additional extension fee of INR 5,000 per additional quarter or part thereof. The Bank may issue LCs in various formats including sight LCs (payment upon document presentation), usance LCs (payment deferred 60-180 days), irrevocable LCs, and revolving LCs for repetitive purchases.

**Letter of Credit Amendment Fee:** INR 6,500 per amendment

If the client wishes to modify any terms of an issued LC (such as increasing amount, extending validity, changing payment terms, adding documents, modifying beneficiary details, etc.), the Bank charges an amendment fee of INR 6,500 per amendment request. Each amendment requires preparation of amendment document, re-verification of terms, and SWIFT transmission to the advising bank.

Multiple changes requested simultaneously are treated as a single amendment. Amendments requested separately on different dates are charged separately. Clients are advised to finalize all LC terms before issuance to minimize amendment costs.

**Letter of Credit Negotiation Fee:** INR 4,500 per document set

When the beneficiary presents trade documents under the LC (such as shipping documents, invoices, bills of exchange, certificates of origin, inspection certificates, etc.), the Bank examines the documents for compliance with LC terms. If documents are compliant, the Bank negotiates the documents (pays the beneficiary or discounts the draft) and charges a negotiation fee of INR 4,500 per document set.

Negotiation includes document receipt and examination, compliance verification against LC terms covering all conditions such as document types, quantities, dates, and descriptions, payment arrangement to the beneficiary, and settlement with the client. Negotiation fees are charged regardless of document compliance status.

**Letter of Credit Discrepancy Handling Fee:** INR 5,000 per LC

If documents presented under an LC are found to be discrepant (not matching LC terms due to errors, omissions, or inconsistencies), the Bank charges a discrepancy handling fee of INR 5,000 per LC to manage the discrepancy. Discrepancy handling includes documenting all discrepancies found, seeking the client's approval to accept discrepant documents, communicating with the issuing bank for waiver of discrepancies, arranging payment subject to client approval, and settling the transaction.

Common discrepancies include late presentation of documents, document dates inconsistent with LC requirements, description of goods not matching LC, missing required documents, and signature/certification issues. Discrepancy fees are charged in addition to negotiation fees.

### 5.2 Bank Guarantees

**Bank Guarantee Issuance Fee:** INR 14,500 per guarantee (one-year tenor)

The Bank issues Bank Guarantees on behalf of clients as a commitment to beneficiaries (typically suppliers, contractors, or tender authorities) that the Bank will pay if the client defaults on its obligations. The guarantee issuance fee of INR 14,500 applies to guarantees with a one-year tenor.

Guarantee types include Performance Guarantees securing contractor performance under construction or service contracts typically up to 24 months with annual renewal options, Bid Bonds or Tender Guarantees for tender participation representing earnest money, Financial Guarantees or Advance Payment Guarantees securing advance payments made by the client's customer, and Deferred Payment Guarantees for credit terms.

**Multi-Year Guarantee Fee:** INR 3,000 per additional year

For guarantees exceeding one year tenor, the Bank charges an additional annual fee of INR 3,000 per additional year or part thereof. For example, a 30-month guarantee is charged INR 14,500 (first year) plus INR 6,000 (second and third years) for a total of INR 20,500.

**Guarantee Amendment Fee:** INR 3,500 per amendment

Amendments to issued guarantees (such as extending tenor, increasing amount, reducing exposure, modifying beneficiary details, changing claim conditions, etc.) are charged at INR 3,500 per amendment request. Early cancellation or release of a guarantee before scheduled expiry is charged INR 2,500.

## 6. DIGITAL BANKING AND TECHNOLOGY SERVICES

### 6.1 Online Banking Portal Access

**Online Portal Monthly Fee:** INR 1,500 per month

The Bank provides access to a secure online banking portal available 24/7 through web browser (https://portal.icicibank.com) and mobile applications for iOS and Android devices. The monthly portal fee of INR 1,500 covers 24/7 portal availability with 99.5% uptime SLA, transaction processing including fund transfers, ACH submissions, RTGS/NEFT initiations, real-time account balance inquiry and transaction history, downloadable statements and reports in PDF and Excel formats, two-factor authentication security, and customer support via chat and email.

Through the online portal, clients can initiate domestic and international fund transfers, submit ACH and RTGS transactions, set up standing instructions, apply for LCs and bank guarantees, upload supporting documents, download statements and reports, monitor account balances and transaction status in real-time, manage user access and permissions for multiple users, and view dashboards with account summary, recent transactions, and upcoming payments.

### 6.2 Automated Sweep Account Management

**Sweep Account Monthly Fee:** INR 3,500 per month

The Bank provides automated sweep account management services for liquidity optimization and interest maximization. The sweep service automatically transfers excess funds from the current account to a term deposit account (sweep-in) to earn interest, and automatically transfers funds back to the current account when needed for payments (sweep-out).

The monthly sweep fee of INR 3,500 covers daily liquidity monitoring with automated threshold management, automated fund transfers based on pre-defined thresholds set by the client, interest optimization with daily interest calculation on swept amounts, daily sweep activity reporting showing sweep-in and sweep-out transactions, and relationship manager support for threshold optimization and strategy.

Clients set minimum and maximum balance thresholds. When the current account balance exceeds the maximum threshold, excess funds are automatically swept to a term deposit. When the current account balance falls below the minimum threshold, funds are automatically swept back from the term deposit to the current account.

### 6.3 API Integration for System-to-System Connectivity

**API Setup Fee (One-Time):** INR 35,000

For clients requiring direct system-to-system integration enabling their ERP, accounting system, or treasury management system to directly submit transactions to the Bank without manual online portal access, the Bank provides API (Application Programming Interface) integration.

The one-time API setup fee of INR 35,000 covers API development and configuration based on client system specifications, API testing in the Bank's sandbox environment, security certification including encryption, authentication, and authorization setup, technical documentation and API specification manuals, and initial training for client technical team (up to 30 days of support).

API integration supports RESTful APIs with JSON payloads, OAuth 2.0 authentication, 256-bit SSL encryption, webhook callbacks for transaction status, and rate limiting based on client tier.

**API Transaction Fee:** INR 2.50 per transaction

After the one-time setup, the Bank charges INR 2.50 per API-based transaction for ongoing usage. This fee applies to each transaction submitted via API including fund transfers, ACH submissions, RTGS/NEFT transactions, balance inquiries, and statement retrievals. API transaction fees are charged monthly based on total API usage during the month.

High-volume API clients processing more than 50,000 API transactions per month may be eligible for volume-based discounts on the per-transaction fee subject to relationship manager approval.

### 6.4 Management Information System (MIS) Reports

**Standard MIS Reports:** Included in online portal fee

Standard MIS reports including daily liquidity report showing opening balance, total inflows, total outflows, and closing balance, weekly payment summary showing payment count and total amount by payment type, and monthly transaction analysis showing transaction volume trends and average transaction size are included in the online portal monthly fee at no additional charge.

**Custom MIS Reports:** INR 2,000 per report

Additional customized MIS reports beyond the standard reports are charged at INR 2,000 per report. Custom reports may include beneficiary-wise payment analysis, vendor-wise transaction summary, category-wise expense analysis, multi-account consolidated reporting, cash flow forecasting reports, and custom pivot tables with user-defined dimensions and metrics.

## 7. CREDIT FACILITIES AND FINANCING SERVICES

### 7.1 Cash Credit Facility

**Cash Credit Interest Rate:** Base Rate + Spread (currently 8.00% + 2.00-3.00% = 10.00-11.00% per annum)

The Bank provides Cash Credit Facilities for short-term working capital requirements with revolving credit limits. Interest is calculated on the daily outstanding balance at the applicable rate which is Base Rate (currently 8.00% per annum) plus client-specific spread (typically 2.00% to 3.00% per annum) depending on credit assessment.

Interest is calculated daily and debited monthly to the client's account. The Bank conducts quarterly reviews of credit facilities with annual renewal subject to satisfactory account conduct and updated financial statements.

**Cash Credit Annual Review Fee:** INR 10,000 per annum

The Bank charges an annual review fee of INR 10,000 for reviewing and renewing the Cash Credit Facility covering credit assessment, financial statement analysis, limit review, and documentation updates.

### 7.2 Trade Credit Line

**Trade Credit Interest Rate:** Base Rate + Spread (currently 8.00% + 1.50-2.50% = 9.50-10.50% per annum)

The Bank provides Trade Credit Lines for financing of invoices and receivables at an interest rate of Base Rate plus spread. Coverage is available up to 80-95% of invoice face value depending on buyer creditworthiness and payment history. Interest is calculated on the financed amount from the date of financing until repayment.

### 7.3 Overdraft Facility

**Overdraft Interest Rate:** 11.00-12.00% per annum on daily overdrawn balance

The Bank provides Overdraft Facilities for temporary liquidity management during seasonal peaks or cash flow mismatches. Interest is calculated daily on the overdrawn amount at the applicable rate (typically 11.00-12.00% per annum). A minimum monthly usage fee of INR 5,000 applies even if the overdraft is not utilized.

## 8. FEE ESCALATION AND MODIFICATION POLICY

### 8.1 Annual Fee Escalation

**Escalation Rate:** 4.0% per annum effective January 1 each year

All fees specified in this catalogue are subject to annual escalation at the rate of 4.0% per annum effective January 1 of each year. The escalation is applied to all fees including account maintenance, transaction fees, trade finance charges, and digital services fees.

For example, if the current account maintenance fee is INR 3,500 per month in 2025, it will be escalated to INR 3,640 per month effective January 1, 2026 (3,500 × 1.04).

Clients with executed banking service agreements may have different escalation terms as specified in their individual agreements. This catalogue represents the Bank's standard escalation policy for new clients and clients without executed agreements.

### 8.2 Fee Modification Authority

The Bank reserves the right to modify fees, introduce new charges, or discontinue existing services with 30 days prior written notice to clients. Fee modifications may be necessitated by changes in regulatory requirements, operational costs, market conditions, or strategic pricing decisions.

Clients will be notified of fee changes via email to the registered email address, notification on the online banking portal, and physical letter to the registered address. Clients may choose to terminate the banking relationship within 30 days of fee change notification without penalty.

## 9. PAYMENT TERMS AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 9.1 Fee Payment and Invoicing

All fees are calculated monthly based on services utilized and transactions processed during the preceding calendar month (1st to last day of the month). Fee invoices are issued within 5 business days of month-end itemizing all charges service-wise with transaction counts and volumes.

Payment of all fees is due within 15 days of the invoice date. The Bank may auto-debit fees from the client's primary operating account maintained with the Bank without requiring separate authorization for each fee debit.

### 9.2 Late Payment Interest

If fees remain unpaid beyond the due date, the client is liable to pay interest on overdue amounts at the rate of 12.0% per annum calculated on a daily basis from the due date until the payment date. Late payment interest is charged monthly and added to the outstanding balance.

### 9.3 Dispute Resolution

Any disputes or discrepancies in fee invoices must be reported by the client in writing to the Bank within 30 days of the invoice date. The Bank shall investigate the dispute and provide resolution within 10 business days. Fee adjustments or reversals shall be applied to the client's next invoice upon the Bank's approval of the dispute claim.

No interest accrues on disputed amounts pending resolution. If a dispute is found in the client's favor, the Bank will refund or credit the disputed amount with interest at the applicable savings account rate for the dispute period.
